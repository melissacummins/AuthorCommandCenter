# How to Mine & Edit Romance Excerpts for Meta Ads

A step-by-step guide for finding the best excerpts in a manuscript and editing them to pass
Meta's censors.

## Is an Excerpt Ad Right for This Book?

- Excerpt ads work best for steamy, dark, and "touch her and die" romance
- The steamier the book, the more likely an excerpt will convert
- For sweet romance, excerpt ads are generally less effective — premise hooks and trope
  signals tend to work better

## Step 1: Mining the Manuscript

You don't need to read the entire manuscript to find great excerpt hooks. Use targeted
searches:

**The "Kiss" Search:** Search the manuscript for the word "kiss." The scenes around kisses
almost always contain the best ad material — flirty banter, tension, the moment right before
or after.

**Hero's POV Moments:** Search for scenes where the hero looks at the heroine and thinks
about everything he loves about her. Romance readers eat these up.

**Flirty Banter:** Look for rapid-fire dialogue exchanges with push-pull energy — teasing,
challenging, the tension before someone makes a move.

**Genre Flavor Moments:** Find the scene that best represents THIS book's specific flavor.
For dark romance, look for the first aggressive, enemies-to-lovers moment. For paranormal,
look for the first supernatural reveal. For monster romance, look for the "she wasn't afraid"
moment.

**Spicy Scenes:** Sex scenes can absolutely be used in ads IF edited carefully to pass
Meta's censors (see Step 3 below).

**Other search terms to try:** "breath" (catches tension moments), "eyes" (catches
attraction moments), "hands" (catches physical tension), "whisper" (catches intimate
moments), character names in close proximity.

## Step 2: Structuring the Excerpt

**Length:** The sweet spot for an excerpt ad is 200-500 words. You can test longer, but
this range gives enough to hook without losing mobile readers.

**The Opening Hook:** Only the first few lines show before the "See more" button. Open on
a strong hook that grabs attention with zero context needed. Examples:
- "I know you slept with my brother."
- "If you get hurt, I will punish you."
- "She told the beast to stop holding back."

**The Cliffhanger:** NEVER end the excerpt at a chapter break or a moment of resolution.
End at a place where the reader desperately needs to know what happens next:
- "He pushes her up against the wall and her breath hitches."
- "And then she smiled. The kind of smile that starts wars."
- "He pulled back just enough to look at her, and what she saw in his eyes..."

The reader should feel like they MUST click the link to find out what happens.

## Step 3: Editing for Meta's Censors

Put the selected excerpt into a clean document and search for every word that will get the
ad rejected.

### Profanity — Edit Out, Stay In Character

- Remove all swear words entirely
- Replace with words that keep the character's voice intact — a mafia boss wouldn't say
  "golly" or "oh my gosh"
- If you can't replace the profanity in-character, remove that line and adjust the text
  around it
- **NEVER use obfuscation** (s***, f@ck, special characters to censor) — this triggers
  immediate rejection AND account flagging
- "Unalived" is allowed

### Anatomy — Remove All References

- Meta's filter instantly rejects: "breast," "nipple," "tit"
- Softening doesn't work either — "nub," "bosom," and "flesh" in breast contexts also fail
- All breast play must be completely removed from the excerpt
- **What DOES pass:** "his body," "his length," "his flesh," "his skin" (in non-breast
  contexts)

### Violence — Rewrite for Emotion, Not Action

- Don't describe specific violent acts (shooting, stabbing, punching, beating)
- Rewrite to convey the EMOTION without the specific act
- "Touch her and die energy" works. "He shot him in the chest" does not.
- Reference the protectiveness, the threat, the consequence — not the mechanics

## Step 4: The CTA After an Excerpt

After the excerpt, transition naturally into the standard CTA. Don't break the mood with
jarring marketing language. A good excerpt ad flows:

[Excerpt with cliffhanger ending]

[Book title] by [Author]. [Genre signal in one line.]

[Trope list]

[Social proof]

[CTA matching ad destination]
