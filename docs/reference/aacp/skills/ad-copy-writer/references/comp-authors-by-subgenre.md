# Finding Comp Authors for Your Ads

Comp authors in your Meta ad description help Facebook's Andromeda algorithm match your ad to
the right readers. The key is finding authors whose readers overlap with YOUR specific book —
not just your genre broadly.

## How to Find Your Comps

### Method 1: Amazon Also-Boughts (Best starting point)

Go to your book's Amazon page (or a book very similar to yours). Scroll to "Customers who
bought this item also bought." Those authors are your comps — their readers literally bought
a book like yours.

If your book isn't published yet, find 2-3 books that are similar to yours and check THEIR
also-boughts.

### Method 2: Amazon Category Bestsellers

Find the most specific Amazon category your book fits in (not just "Romance" — dig into
sub-sub-categories like "Paranormal > Vampires" or "Romantic Suspense > Mafia"). The top
authors in that category are potential comps.

### Method 3: Goodreads Shelves

Search Goodreads for your book's tropes (e.g., "dark mafia romance"). Look at which authors
appear repeatedly on those shelves. Also check "Listopia" lists like "Best Dark Romance" —
the authors who appear on the same lists as your book are natural comps.

### Method 4: BookTok / Bookstagram

Search TikTok and Instagram for your trope hashtags (#darkromance, #monsterromance, etc.).
Which authors get recommended most often? Those authors have active social audiences that
overlap with your readers.

### Method 5: Ask Your Readers

If you have an existing readership: "If you love my books, who else do you read?" This is
gold for finding comps you might not have considered.

## What Makes a Good Comp for Ads

Not every similar author is a good ad comp. The best comps have:

1. **Active Facebook ad presence** — They or their publisher run Meta ads, which means Facebook
   has interest data on their readers. Authors who ONLY market on TikTok won't have strong
   Facebook interest data.
2. **Readership overlap with your SPECIFIC book** — A dark mafia romance with heavy BDSM
   elements has different comps than a dark mafia romance with slow burn and enemies to lovers.
3. **Large enough audience** — Very small indie authors won't have enough Facebook interest
   data to be useful as targeting signals.

## How to Use Comps in Your SEO Block

Don't just list names. Add descriptors that tell Facebook WHY each comp matches:

- "Perfect for fans of Rina Kent's dark mafia worlds" — ties Rina Kent to "dark mafia"
- "Danielle Lori's slow-burn intensity" — ties Danielle Lori to "slow burn"
- "Kresley Cole's witty paranormal worlds" — ties Kresley Cole to "paranormal" + "witty"

These descriptors give the algorithm two signals instead of one: the author's name AND the
quality that connects them to your book.

## Starter Lists by Subgenre

These are well-known authors with large Facebook ad audiences, organized by subgenre. Use
as a starting point, then supplement with your own research.

### Dark Mafia Romance
Rina Kent, Danielle Lori, Penelope Douglas, Nikita Slater, Tillie Cole, Cora Reilly,
Natasha Knight, Zoe Blake, Pam Godwin

### Dark Vampire / Paranormal Romance
Kresley Cole, J.R. Ward, Gena Showalter, Nalini Singh, Sherrilyn Kenyon, Christine Feehan,
Lara Adrian, Lynsay Sands

### Monster / Beast / Creature Romance
Lillian Lark, Cassie Alexander, Layla Fae, Katee Robert, Lia Frost, Eva Brandt, Cara Wylde,
Finley Fenn, Ruby Dixon, Tiffany Roberts

### Contemporary Romance
Colleen Hoover, Emily Henry, Tessa Bailey, Hannah Grace, Ana Huang, Elle Kennedy,
Lauren Asher, Lucy Score

### Romantasy
Sarah J. Maas, Jennifer L. Armentrout, Holly Black, Rebecca Yarros, Elise Kova

### Reverse Harem / Why Choose
C.M. Stunich, Tate James, Caroline Peckham & Susanne Valenti

### Historical Romance
Lisa Kleypas, Julia Quinn, Tessa Dare, Eloisa James

---

**Remember:** This list is a starting point, not a definitive guide. Your book's specific
trope blend matters more than its broad genre. A dark contemporary romance with age gap and
forbidden elements pulls from different comp pools than a dark contemporary with enemies to
lovers and forced proximity. Let the manuscript's specific content guide your comp choices.
