# TikTok/BookTok Hook Research

This reference covers what actually works for TikTok/BookTok video ad hooks for romance books.
These findings are based on research across BookTok guides, indie author TikTok strategies,
and Meta's Andromeda algorithm documentation.

**The core difference from Meta ad hooks:** On TikTok, the hook IS the first frame of text on
screen. There's no separate intro or buildup. It must land in under 2 seconds, ideally 5-7
words. The hook determines whether anyone sees the rest — if viewers swipe away in the first
second, nothing else matters.

## Hook Formulas That Work on BookTok

### 1. Curiosity Gap (Highest-performing)

A single line that creates a question the viewer MUST answer. The gap between what they know
and what they want to know makes it almost impossible to scroll away.

**Formula:** [Subject] + [unexpected action that raises a question]

**Examples:**
- "She told the monster to hunt her." (instant "wait, what?")
- "He saved her life. Then he decided she was his." (asymmetry hook)
- "She didn't know he existed yet. He'd already decided she was his."

**Why it works:** The viewer's brain automatically tries to close the gap. They can't scroll
away without finding out what happened.

### 2. Pattern Interrupt

Flip the expected dynamic. Readers expect the heroine to run FROM the monster/villain. When
the hook reverses that, it breaks the scrolling pattern.

**Formula:** [Expected role] + [does the opposite of what's expected]

**Examples:**
- "She didn't run. She told him to chase her."
- "He was supposed to be the monster. She's the one who bit."
- "The beast begged. She's the one who said no."

**Why it works:** BookTok romance readers have seen hundreds of "he's dangerous, she can't
resist" hooks. A reversal creates novelty that forces a second look.

### 3. Single Devastating Line

One short, emotionally loaded line. BookTok proved that "Touch her and you die" circulated
more powerfully than entire book blurbs.

**Formula:** [Sharp, visceral statement under 8 words]

**Examples:**
- "Touch her and you die."
- "He wasn't gentle. She didn't want him to be."
- "She told him she wasn't worth it."

**Why it works:** Short lines leave maximum room for the viewer's imagination. Less context =
more personal resonance.

### 4. POV Format

Puts the viewer directly in the scene. Works well for immersive, emotionally charged content.

**Formula:** "POV: You [action/situation that puts viewer in heroine's position]"

**Examples:**
- "POV: You tell the beast to stop holding back."
- "POV: He's been watching you for a year. You just found out."
- "POV: The monster who saved your life decides you're his."

**Why it works:** Second-person framing makes it personal. The viewer doesn't just observe
the story — they're IN it.

## Critical Insight: Vulnerability Outsells Spice as a Hook

The hook that stops the scroll is NOT the spiciest line — it's the one with the most emotional
tension.

**What this means in practice:**
- Lead with the heroine's vulnerability, self-doubt, or emotional wound
- Lead with the hero's devotion or possessive tenderness (not just raw dominance)
- Lead with the moment that makes someone FEEL something, not just the moment that's hot
- The spice can be the payoff (the excerpt itself), but the HOOK should create emotional
  curiosity first

**Example comparison:**
- Weak hook: "He pinned her against the wall." (spicy but no emotional stakes)
- Strong hook: "She told him she wasn't worth it." (vulnerability — viewer needs to know
  his response)

## What Does NOT Work as a TikTok Hook

- **Narrative setups longer than one sentence** — Too much context. The viewer is gone.
- **Telling instead of showing** — "This is a monster romance about a gargoyle who finds his
  mate." That's a blurb, not a hook.
- **Generic romance energy** — "He's dangerous and she can't resist him." Could be any of
  10,000 books.
- **Any hook that takes more than 2 seconds to read on screen**

## Excerpt-Over-Background Video Format

A manuscript excerpt appears as text overlay on an atmospheric background video. One of the
most effective low-effort ad formats for BookTok romance.

### Structure

| Time | Element |
|------|---------|
| 0-2s | **Hook text** — the scroll-stopper line, alone on screen |
| 2-8s | **Excerpt text** — appearing line by line with key words highlighted in a contrasting color |
| 8-10s | **Book cover + title** — clean end card |
| 8-10s | **Trope stack** (optional, on end card) — 3-5 tropes with emoji bullets |

### Background Selection

The background should create atmosphere WITHOUT competing with the text for attention.

**Best backgrounds for dark/monster romance:** Dark forest footage, rain on windows, candlelight,
slow atmospheric nature footage, abstract dark textures (smoke, ink in water).

**Also works (the "satisfying random" approach):** Soap cutting, wax melting, paint mixing —
attention-holding backgrounds that don't compete with text.

**Avoid:** Anything too visually busy, anything bright or high-contrast that makes text hard
to read.

### Text Highlighting

Highlight emotionally charged words in a different color or bold weight:
- For possessive/claiming scenes: highlight "mine," "keep," "never let go"
- For chase/primal scenes: highlight "chase," "hunt," "monster," "beast"
- For tender/devotion scenes: highlight "safe," "worth it," "enough," "always"

### Sound

85% of TikTok/Meta users watch with sound off. Design the video to work silently. If adding
audio, use trending atmospheric sounds or dark moody instrumentals — never voiceover that
replaces the text.

## Meta Algorithm Considerations (Andromeda)

Meta's algorithm reads text overlays via OCR and uses them for audience targeting:

- **Trope stacks on end cards help targeting** — The algorithm reads trope keywords and matches
  the ad to users who engage with similar content.
- **Visual Entity Optimization (VEO)** — The algorithm scans images for "nouns" (daggers,
  crowns, wings, claws) and matches them to users who engage with similar imagery.
- **The "Hook, Retain, Reward" formula** — 0-3 seconds is the hook, 3-10 seconds retains
  with the excerpt content, 10+ seconds rewards with the book cover and CTA.
